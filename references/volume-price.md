# Volume-Price Dynamics — Complete Reference

## O'Dea Volume Principle

**Core Principle**: Volume is the fuel that drives price. Price movement without volume confirmation is suspect.

### The Law of Effort vs. Result
- **High volume + large price move** = Effort matches result → valid move.
- **High volume + small price move** = Effort exceeds result → potential reversal (stopping volume).
- **Low volume + large price move** = Result exceeds effort → unsustainable, likely fakeout.
- **Low volume + small price move** = No conviction → continuation of prior trend.

## Key Volume-Price Patterns

### 1. Breakout Explosion (起涨点)

The most important pattern for entry timing.

**Criteria:**
1. Price closes above a well-defined resistance level (prior swing high, trendline, moving average).
2. Volume on the breakout day ≥ 120% of the 20-day average volume (+20% minimum).
3. The breakout candle should close near its high (not a shooting star or doji on the breakout day).
4. Ideally, the breakout level has been tested at least 2-3 times previously.

**Confirmation:**
- Next 1-3 days must hold above the breakout level (no "failed breakout").
- Volume should remain elevated, not collapse immediately.
- The breakout level becomes new support.

### 2. Low-Level Repeated Test (首低反复测量)

A basing pattern that precedes breakouts.

**Criteria:**
1. Price revisits a prior swing low.
2. On the retest, volume contracts by ≥ 8% compared to the volume at the previous low.
3. Price closes above the prior low (higher low established).
4. This indicates selling pressure is exhausted.

**Signal Strength:**
- Single retest with -8% volume: Moderate signal.
- Double retest with declining volume each time: Strong signal.
- Triple retest (triple bottom) with each vol lower: Very strong signal.

### 3. Stopping Volume (Climax)

**Bullish Stopping Volume (at lows):**
- Massive spike in volume after a prolonged decline.
- Wide-range bar closing at or near the high.
- Indicates capitulation — panic sellers exhausted, smart money absorbing.

**Bearish Stopping Volume (at highs):**
- Massive spike in volume after a prolonged advance.
- Narrow range or reversal bar (closing near the low).
- Indicates distribution — smart money selling into retail enthusiasm.

## Moving Average Framework

### MA Alignment (Trend Confirmation)

```
BULLISH:   5MA > 10MA > 20MA > 30MA > 60MA
BEARISH:   5MA < 10MA < 20MA < 30MA < 60MA
CHOPPY:    MAs intertwined → no clear trend
```

### Key Crossovers

| Crossover | Signal | Reliability |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| 5MA crosses above 10MA | Short-term bullish | Low (frequent whipsaws) |
| 10MA crosses above 30MA | Intermediate bullish | Medium |
| 10MA crosses above ssMA (60MA/120MA) | Major uptrend confirmed | High |
| 10MA crosses below ssMA | Major downtrend (Death Cross) | High |

### Moving Average as Dynamic Support/Resistance

- In uptrends, pullbacks to the 20MA or 30MA are common entry points.
- A close below the 30MA is the first warning signal.
- Multiple consecutive closes below the 30MA invalidates the short-term uptrend.

## MACD Framework

### Configuration
- Fast EMA: 12
- Slow EMA: 26
- Signal Line: 9
- Histogram = MACD Line - Signal Line

### Key Signals (Ranked by Reliability)

| Signal | Description | Reliability |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| **Zero-Line Golden Cross** | MACD crosses above signal line while both are above zero | HIGHEST — confirms uptrend acceleration |
| **Low-Zone Second Golden Cross** | MACD makes a higher low and crosses above signal near or below zero | HIGH — reversal signal after deep correction |
| **High-Zone Death Cross** | MACD crosses below signal line while both are high above zero | HIGH — trend exhaustion |
| **Bullish Divergence** | Price makes lower low, MACD makes higher low | MEDIUM-HIGH — reversal signal |
| **Bearish Divergence** | Price makes higher high, MACD makes lower high | HIGH — trend exhaustion, imminent correction |
| **Zero-Line Death Cross** | MACD crosses below signal while both are near zero | MEDIUM — resumption of downtrend |

### Divergence Deep-Dive

**Bearish Divergence (Most Important Warning Signal):**
```
Price:  Higher High  →  Higher High
MACD:   Higher High  →  LOWER High  ← DIVERGENCE
Action: Take profits / tighten stops / prepare for reversal
```

**Hidden Bullish Divergence (Continuation):**
```
Price:  Higher Low
MACD:   Lower Low    ← Higher low in price vs lower low in MACD
Action: Continuation of uptrend — add to position
```

## Fakeout Detection Protocol

### At Low Price Zones
- Breakout on **expanding volume** → likely genuine.
- Breakout on **low/declining volume** → likely fakeout (institutional paint job).
- Must see volume ≥ +20% vs 20-day average.

### At High Price Zones (After Extended Rally)
- **Any breakout** (regardless of volume) in a high price zone carries elevated fakeout risk.
- Institutions may push price to new highs on declining volume specifically to trap retail buyers.
- Trust the divergences — if MACD/RSI diverge while price makes new highs, the breakout is likely terminal.

### Volume Confirmation Checklist
- [ ] Volume on breakout day ≥ 1.2x 20-day average?
- [ ] Volume sustained (not one-day spike that collapses)?
- [ ] Price closes near the session high (not a reversal candle)?
- [ ] No bearish MACD divergence present?
- [ ] MA alignment remains bullish?

If ≥ 4 of 5 pass → Genuine breakout. If ≤ 2 pass → Fakeout warning.

## Practical Volume-Price Scanning

When scanning for setups, look for this sequence:

1. **Accumulation Phase**: Low, declining volume. Price range-bound. Low-Level Repeated Tests with contracting volume.
2. **Spring / Shakeout**: Brief dip below support on light volume, quickly recovered. Clears out weak hands.
3. **Breakout**: Price breaks above resistance on expanding volume (+20%). 5MA > 10MA > 30MA.
4. **Backtest (Golden Entry)**: Price retests the breakout level. Volume contracts on the retest. Price holds.
5. **Advance**: Price moves toward Fibonacci extension targets.

The highest-probability entry is at Step 4 (the backtest/retest of the breakout level).
