# 100-Bagger & 10-Bagger Genetics — Complete Reference

## SQGLP Framework for 100-Bagger Identification

Inspired by Thomas Phelps' "100 to 1 in the Stock Market" and Peter Lynch's growth frameworks, the SQGLP model provides a systematic screen for stocks with 100x return potential over a 15-25 year horizon.

### S — Size (Starting Market Capitalization)

**Rule**: The smaller the starting base, the higher the multiplicative ceiling.

| Market Cap at Launch | 100x Target Cap | Feasibility |
|---------------------|-----------------|-------------|
| < ¥1B (micro-cap)   | < ¥100B         | HIGH        |
| ¥1B - ¥3B           | ¥100B - ¥300B   | MEDIUM-HIGH |
| ¥3B - ¥10B          | ¥300B - ¥1T     | MEDIUM      |
| ¥10B - ¥30B         | ¥1T - ¥3T       | LOW         |
| > ¥30B              | > ¥3T           | VERY LOW    |

**Key Insight**: 100-baggers almost always start as small companies. A ¥1T company becoming ¥100T is exponentially harder than a ¥1B company becoming ¥100B.

### Q — Quality (Business & Management)

**Business Quality Indicators:**
1. **Return on Invested Capital (ROIC) > 15%** consistently over multiple years.
2. **Moat (Sustainable Competitive Advantage)**:
   - Brand power (pricing power above inflation).
   - Network effects (each additional user increases value for all).
   - High switching costs (customers locked in).
   - Cost advantages (scale, location, technology).
   - Regulatory barriers (licenses, patents).
3. **Capital-Light Model**: Can grow without massive incremental capital investment.

**Management Quality Indicators:**
- Insider ownership > 10% (aligned incentives).
- Long-tenured leadership (founder-led preferred).
- Conservative accounting (no aggressive revenue recognition).
- Capital allocation skill (smart M&A, buybacks at undervaluation, reinvestment at high ROIC).
- No history of dilution for non-value-creating purposes.

### G — Growth (Earnings Growth Rate)

**The "Good Growth" Test**:
- Revenue AND EPS must BOTH grow. Growth from dilution is fake growth.
- Organic revenue growth > industry average.
- EPS growth tracks or exceeds revenue growth.

**Sustained Compound Growth Math:**
| Annual Growth | Years to 10x | Years to 100x |
|---------------|-------------|---------------|
| 15%           | 16.4        | 32.9          |
| 20%           | 12.6        | 25.3          |
| 25%           | 10.3        | 20.6          |
| 30%           | 8.8         | 17.6          |

**Growth Quality Check:**
- Is growth accelerating or decelerating?
- Is the TAM (Total Addressable Market) large enough to support 20%+ growth for 20+ years?
- Are margins improving with scale, or deteriorating?

### L — Longevity (Sustainability of Q and G)

**Durability Assessment:**
1. **Industry Tailwind Duration**: Is this a multi-decade structural shift (cloud computing, AI, aging population) or a cyclical boom?
2. **Competitive Position Stability**: Is the moat widening or narrowing?
3. **Reinvestment Runway**: Can the company redeploy earnings at high ROIC for decades?

**Red Flags for Longevity:**
- Industry undergoing rapid technological disruption.
- Regulatory risk that could invalidate the business model.
- Dependency on a single customer, product, or geography.
- Key person risk (single founder with no succession plan).

### P — Price (Entry Valuation)

**The Paradox of 100-Bagger Entry:**
- Great 100-baggers are rarely "cheap" by traditional metrics at their entry point.
- A stock trading at 40x earnings that compounds at 25% for 20 years → 100x return.
- A stock trading at 8x earnings that compounds at 5% for 20 years → 2.6x return.

**Valuation Framework:**
```
PEG Ratio = P/E Ratio / Earnings Growth Rate (%)

Rule of thumb for growth stocks:
  PEG < 1.0  → Potentially undervalued
  PEG 1.0-1.5 → Fair value
  PEG 1.5-2.0 → Premium, but acceptable for exceptional quality
  PEG > 2.0   → Requires extraordinary growth to justify
```

**Entry Timing**: The best entry isn't at the absolute low. It's when:
- The business model is proven but not yet fully appreciated.
- Growth is accelerating (not decelerating).
- The industry narrative is shifting from "speculative" to "structural".

## 10-Bagger Specific Profile (A-Share Market)

China's A-share market has produced numerous 10-baggers with common characteristics:

### Profile Checklist
- [ ] **Starting Market Cap**: < ¥3B (¥30亿) at the beginning of the 10x journey.
- [ ] **Industry Stage**: Born in the early high-growth phase of a new industry (EV batteries 2019, solar 2020, AI 2023).
- [ ] **Era Context**: Riding a generational shift — policy support, technological breakthrough, or consumption upgrade.
- [ ] **Sector Liberalization**: Often emerges when state monopolies are broken open and private capital enters previously restricted sectors (education, healthcare, finance).
- [ ] **Restructuring Catalyst**: Asset injections, backdoor listings, or business model pivots that transform the earnings profile overnight.

### Common 100-Bagger DNA Patterns

1. **Compounding Machines**: Steady 20%+ growth for 20+ years. Boring businesses that quietly dominate.
2. **Inflection Point Riders**: A small company at the exact moment its industry explodes. The TAM expands faster than competitors can enter.

## Genetic Scoring Matrix

Use this to score any stock's 100-bagger potential:

| Dimension | Score 1-3 (Weak) | Score 4-6 (Moderate) | Score 7-8 (Strong) | Score 9-10 (Exceptional) |
|-----------|------------------|----------------------|--------------------|---------------------------|
| **S** | > ¥30B market cap | ¥10B-30B | ¥3B-10B | < ¥3B |
| **Q** | ROIC < 10%, no moat | ROIC 10-15%, emerging moat | ROIC 15-20%, clear moat | ROIC > 20%, unassailable moat |
| **G** | < 10% revenue CAGR | 10-15% CAGR | 15-20% CAGR | > 20% CAGR, accelerating |
| **L** | Industry in decline | Stable mature industry | Growing industry, 5+ yr runway | Structural tailwind, 10+ yr runway |
| **P** | PEG > 3.0 | PEG 2.0-3.0 | PEG 1.0-2.0 | PEG < 1.0 OR exceptional quality justifies premium |

**Composite Scoring:**
- **8.0 - 10.0**: Strong 100-bagger candidate. High conviction.
- **6.0 - 7.9**: Possible 10-bagger. Monitor for improving fundamentals.
- **4.0 - 5.9**: Speculative. Requires catalyst for thesis to play out.
- **Below 4.0**: Pass. Better opportunities exist elsewhere.
